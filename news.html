Recent as of Wednesday, March 28, 2018 - Today's the day we take things back to the way they should've been. And I don't mean we take the Minecraft server back to its roots. I mean we take the thing that led to us launching the server back to its roots.

Nosiphus' original purpose post-December 2, 2012 was to be the company that coded and updated the Saturn operating system. The Minecraft server existed to be the sandbox for future ideas of what Nosiphus might become, with possible ideas for a corporate headquarters as well as the testing ground for ideas for Saturn.

As time went on, the computer application development division of Nosiphus became less and less important as the server and YouTube increasingly took over the company. In late 2013, Saturn, instead of actually being a programmed operating system, was simply a customized distribution of openSUSE Linux. We want to be able to write our own kernel and codebase, as difficult as it will be.

From today, we plan to work on Saturn as a full system environment once again. We have some designs down, and we will be working on this over the next few years. This does not mean we will be abandoning Minecraft. On the contrary, Telkit will continue with its planned transition path.
